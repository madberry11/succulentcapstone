Introduction

Succulents are plants that have parts that are more than normally thickened and more fleshy in order to store water. They tend to come from arid, hot climates where water is scarce. Recently succulents are increasingly popular due to their cute looks and being marketed as plants that do not require a green thumb to keep alive.

While cacti are by definition succulents, they tend to be referred to separately.

Sun

Succulents thrive in sunny, well-drained places. If the weather is suitable, they can be kept outdoors, possibly even all year around. If you live in a location that is too cold, too dark or too wet (or if you live in lovely Scotland like myself, all three of them) your succulents have a better chance at surviving indoors.

Unfortunately during winter sometimes even the sunniest windowsill is not enough. You will know your succulent is not getting enough light if it suddenly starts to grow tall with big gaps between leaves. If this happens, you may need to get some grow lights to make up for the lack of sunshine.
